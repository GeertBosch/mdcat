# Math expressions

LaTeX math between `$...$` (inline) and `$$ ... $$` (block) is transliterated to
Unicode on a best-effort basis. Anything that can't be mapped is left literal.

## Scientific notation

The Avogadro constant is about $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ per mole, and a tiny mass
might be $1.38 \times 10^{-38}$ kg.

## Greek letters

Lowercase: $\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta, \epsilon, \theta, \lambda, \mu, \pi,
\sigma, \phi, \omega$.

Uppercase: $\Gamma, \Delta, \Theta, \Lambda, \Sigma, \Phi, \Psi, \Omega$.

## Symbols and relations

For all $x \in \mathbb{R}$, $\exists y$ such that $x \perp y$. We have
$A \subset B$, $B \supset C$, and $x \to \infty$. Also $a \simeq b$,
$p \parallel q$, $\therefore x$, and $\because y$.

## Sub- and superscripts

Water is $H_2O$; a power is $x^2$; an indexed power is $a^{1.2}$; multiple
digits work too: $x_{13}$ and $y^{42}$.

## Styled text

Units stay upright with $\mathrm{km/h}$, and vectors are bold:
$\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{w}$.

## Block math

$$E = mc^2 \quad \therefore m = E \cdot c^{-2}$$

## Not math

These dollar amounts are left alone: I paid $5 for lunch and $12 for dinner.
